Last — the agent itself. Templates wire up the harness for you — Claude Code, pi.dev, or roll your own. Give it a name and toggle which connections and capabilities it's allowed to use — every agent's reach is scoped explicitly. Save.

Underneath, Dam is spinning up a fresh pod, mounting the credentials you scoped, and bringing up the harness inside it. Network egress is locked to the connections you allowed — nothing else is reachable. When the badge flips green, the agent's ready. Click in.

Now real work. Check the Dam repository — who changed what this week, write me a markdown digest, save it. Send, and wait for the agent's result.

The agent runs inside the pod. No shell on your machine, nothing to clean up afterwards. When something needs your call, an approval pops in your inbox — answer it, the agent continues. Your laptop can sleep; the pod keeps going. The session lives on the platform, reachable from your phone — or anyone on the team you've shared access with.

Generated files land right here. Open one — the digest, right in the panel. Edit it, or add your own. The workspace is a volume that outlives the pod — your edits are there the next time the agent wakes up.
